The Five-Year Window

Between 1968 and 1973 — five years — the following events occurred simultaneously across six inde[20?]pendent domains:

Family Structure

California legalized no-fault divorce (1969). Griswold and Eisenstadt decoupled sex from marriage through contraceptive access[21?](1965, 1972). Roe v. Wade legalized abortion nationwide (1973). The divorce rate began its vertical climb toward a peak of 5.2 per thousand.[23]

Institutional Trust

The Vietnam War peaked and exposed. The Pentagon Papers drop[24?]ped (1971). Kent State happened (1970). Watergate broke (1972). Trust in government fell from 62% to 36% in four years.[25]

Moral Standards

The Hays Code collapsed and was replaced by the MPAA ratingsystem (1968). The Miller Test codified moral relativism into obscenity[27?]law (1973). Deep Throat entered mainstream theaters (1972). Playboy circulation hit 7 million, up 536% from 1965.[26]

Religious Authority

Christian identification began its long slide from 87% (1973[28]) toward 45% (2023). Church attendance dropped below 40%. The[30]APA removed homosexuality from the DSM (December 15, 1973).[29]

Language and Culture

"All in the Family" premiered with taboo language on broadcast TV (1971). The Controlled Substances Act formalized the drug war (197[31?]0). Profanity began its measurable migration from taboo to normal.

Economic Structure

Nixon took the dollar off the g[32?]old standard (1971). The relationship between currency and physical reality severed. Planned obsolescence became the dominant manufacturing philosophy.
